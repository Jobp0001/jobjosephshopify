Dwindle is an app to trace your Carbon Foot print – and moreover help you make a real change in Climate Change.It is a social Network , that means while it lets you trace and track your Carbon footprint it also lets you share that information with other users of the service. The word 'Dwindle' means to diminish gradually in size, amount, or strength. Each person who uses this app is called a 'Dwindler'.

All Dwindlers have a Green Score or G-score for short. If you have lesser Carbon footprint your G-Score increases. So while using the App users are trying to gain G-Scores by saving energy, and reducing their carbon foorprint in day-to-day activities.

Internet and Online Shops

The Shopify Video about package delivery gave me a new direction to think.What is the Carbon footprint of the Interne? And as an answer to that question I learned that the internet is responsible for roughly one billion tonnes of greenhouse gases a year. And online stores, they have a major impact on the environemt. Packaging and trasportation are the main contributors of greenhouse gases.

And these days we have everything, including food delivered to us at our doorstep. People are getting used a lifestyle of the comfort that online shopping and trade offers.

The Last Mile

Almost quarter of the transportation footprint comes from medium- and heavy-duty trucks.The increasing impact is coming in what people in the world of supply-chain logistics call “The Last mile,” meaning the final stretch from a distribution center to a package’s destination.The last mile can be range from a dozen to one hundred miles. Sometimes trucks travel in a direction for miles to deliver a small package to a specific doorstep. At times packages to the same destination ordered on the same date is delivered over multiple days. all of these increase lat mile journeys.

The most commonly suggested solution is to use 'Green Trucks' that uses clean fuel. It is already happening and we will see more green trucks in the future. Another way is to encourage in-store pickup. For both these cases an average online shopper has nothing much to offer. It is upto the giant companies to make a change. But I kept thinking, as there must be a way for common people to bring change and that is also what created this app.

The Dwindle App has two key features or working modes:

1. Your personal carbonfootprint tracking, for energy management, like electricty for instance. It uses IOT and when in your home wi-fi connected to the weather monitoring networks to give you a notofication like this:

“It sunny outside, turn of lights and open a window?”

2. Green Drives : To share a ride with dwindlers or help each other to deliver packages, thereby reducing lastmile. Lets assume that a user is traveling from Ottawa to Toronto, so he/she will upload this information ( if he/she needs to) atleast a day before. The app searches through the network of dwindlers and matches 'Active rides' that is others who are traveling in the direction or who needs to send or recive a package traveliing in that direction. So basicaly users can share a ride or act as a delivery van, thereby reducing lastmile.

Although I have heard about climate change and carbon foorprint I never knew that using the internet and shopping online has an impact on the internet. Plus I never knew ‘Last Mile’ was such a big issue. As for the design, the challenge was to find something that normal people can do to actually make a change rather than just changing a few bulbs.

The reason why I made it into a social network is because it brings other people into the picture rather than just a single user tracking his/her own carbon footprint. The idea behind G-score is to make the app interesting for dwindlers.
